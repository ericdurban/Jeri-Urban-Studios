Is piano or flute the right instrument for my child?

Often it is the relationship with the teacher and the experience of the sessions are that are more important for the child than the specific instrument. I prioritize building great relationships with families and creating the optimal learning experience for each student. I am focused on making sessions engaging, fun, and have realistic expectations for each age group. And a great foundation in music is transferable to any instrument!

I am a strong believer in private sessions even if the student is already in band or an ensemble. Private sessions will greatly enhance all the skills that public schools offer, not to mention the level of detail one on one sessions offer are hard to compare to a big group session.

What kind of flute should I purchase?

If your child is younger than 8 or 9 years old, the full size flute is usually too big, so a flute with a curved headjoint is recommended. This shortens the reach and makes it possible for young children to play comfortably. There are several different brands and models to choose from. How much do we have to practice?

Practicing outside of session time is essential for making progress on the instrument. I want you to be successful and will help you to develop a good practice routine! I generally recommend that our young beginners start off with 10-15 minutes a couple days a week and work their way up. Students make a practice list at the end of every session with me and I have many resources available to help families make practice fun and enjoyable throughout the week!
