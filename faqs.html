Is piano or flute the right instrument for my child?

Often it is the relationship with the teacher and the experience of the sessions are that are more important for the child than the specific instrument. I prioritize building great relationships with families and creating the optimal learning experience for each student. I am focused on making sessions engaging, fun, and have realistic expectations for each age group. And a great foundation in music is transferable to any instrument!

I am a strong believer in private sessions even if the student is already in band or an ensemble. Private sessions will greatly enhance all the skills that public schools offer, not to mention the level of detail one on one sessions offer are hard to compare to a big group session.

How much do we have to practice?

Practicing outside of session time is essential for making progress on the instrument. I want you to be successful and will help you to develop a good practice routine!

My goal for the students is to practice 20-30 minutes, 5 days a week. They are responsible for tracking their practice time and reporting it to me. A reward system is in place to acknowledge the amount of practice completed.
